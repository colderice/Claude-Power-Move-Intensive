# "I Switched to Claude" — Free Training Blueprint
*Lead gen course for the Facebook group. Feeds the email list. Bridges to Atlanta.*

---

## The Concept

The "I Switched to Claude" Facebook group has momentum because the name does the work — it validates the decision people already made (or are about to make) and positions them inside a tribe. The free training locks that community in place and turns passive members into engaged leads.

**Target audience:** People who just left or are leaving ChatGPT. They have AI literacy. They know what prompting is. They don't know how Claude is different or how to use it right. They want wins fast.

**Dual goal:** Build the email list. Warm the audience for Atlanta.

---

## Course Name

**"The Claude Switch: 6 Sessions to Start Winning with Claude"**

Tagline: *You made the switch. Now make it work.*

This name ties directly to the group name, implies a transformation, and sets clear expectations.

---

## Format

- **Live delivery:** Facebook Group (I Switched to Claude)
- **Repurpose to:** YouTube playlist + recorded course in GHL
- **Cadence:** 2 sessions per week over 3 weeks
- **Session length:** 35-45 min live, trimmed to 25-30 min recorded
- **Style:** Demo-heavy. Show Claude working in real time. Not slides.

---

## Opt-In Funnel (GHL)

```
Person discovers the FB group (organic share, content, ads)
→ Joins the group
→ Pinned post + welcome message: "Get the free 6-part training"
→ Link to GHL landing page (email opt-in to unlock)
→ Confirmation email + link to Session 1
→ Weekly email drip delivers each session link
→ Emails 5-6 pivot hard toward Atlanta event
→ Tagging in GHL: [switched-to-claude] + [webinar-interested] or [event-interested]
```

**Two audiences this builds:**
1. People who will buy the AI Launch Blueprint (~$997) — digital track
2. People who will attend Atlanta — in-person track

Both get the same nurture. You segment by behavior (link clicks, replies, form fills).

## The 6 Sessions

---

### Session 1: "Why You Switched (And Why You're Not Going Back)"

**Purpose:** Validate the decision, set the frame, build trust.

**What you cover:**
- The honest ChatGPT vs Claude comparison — no hype, just what's actually different
- Why Claude feels different: it reasons, it pushes back, it sounds like a human wrote it
- The mindset shift: Claude is not a search engine with a chat box
- Introduce the "AI employee" framing (plant the seed early — don't push, just mention)

**Demo:** Same prompt into ChatGPT vs Claude side by side. Let the output do the talking.

**CTA:** Join the group, share this with one person who's still on the fence.

---

### Session 2: "How Claude Actually Thinks"

**Purpose:** Teach the mechanics. Make people feel like insiders.

**What you cover:**
- Context window — what it means, why it changes everything
- How Claude handles nuance: it reads tone, adjusts to your voice, holds more context
- Projects feature — set it up once, it remembers your business every session
- Why the same prompt hits different in Claude vs other tools

**Demo:** Build a Project live. Set up a business context. Show how it changes the output.

**CTA:** Set up your first Claude Project before the next session. Screenshot it and drop it in the group.

---

### Session 3: "Prompting Claude the Right Way"

**Purpose:** Fix the #1 mistake switchers make — using ChatGPT prompts in Claude.

**What you cover:**
- ChatGPT prompting vs Claude prompting: what has to change
- The four-part Claude prompt structure: Role, Task, Context, Format
- Why Claude doesn't need command-style prompts — it prefers conversation
- Give them a starter prompt library (PDF lead magnet — email capture again here)

**Demo:** Take a real ChatGPT prompt from the audience (collect in advance). Rebuild it for Claude live. Show the difference.

**CTA:** Download the Starter Prompt Library (email opt-in reinforcement or secondary capture for non-subscribers).

---

### Session 4: "Claude's Secret Weapons"

**Purpose:** Show the features ChatGPT either doesn't have or does worse. Create desire and loyalty.

**What you cover:**
- Artifacts — what they are and why they matter for content creators and coaches
- Extended thinking — when and why to use it
- Long document handling — drop in a 50-page PDF and have a conversation with it
- Voice and tone calibration — how to train Claude to write like you

**Demo:** Drop a long document in. Ask Claude specific business questions about it. Show artifacts generating a deliverable in real time.

**CTA:** Drop your most impressive Claude output in the comments. Best one gets featured.

---

### Session 5: "Build Your First Claude Workflow"

**Purpose:** Move from feature education to business application. This is where the "AI employee" concept lands.

**What you cover:**
- What a Claude workflow actually looks like in a real business
- Walk through one workflow from scratch: intake → analysis → output → delivery
- Introduce the concept of AI employees (not just AI tools) — this is the reframe
- Soft introduction to Marblism: "What if Claude was one piece of a whole workforce?"

**Demo:** Build one complete workflow live. Use a real business scenario from the audience.

**CTA:** Marblism affiliate mention (code JOHN, 25% off, $39/month for the whole team). No hard sell — just "this is what I use."

---

### Session 6: "What's Next — From Switched to Unstoppable"

**Purpose:** Close the loop on the training, deliver the final value, hard pivot to Atlanta.

**What you cover:**
- Recap the shift: you didn't just switch tools, you changed how you work
- The difference between using Claude and deploying Claude as part of a system
- Preview what's possible when Claude + Marblism + Genspark work together as a stack
- What the Claude Power Move Intensive in Atlanta actually builds in the room

**Demo:** Show a full AI employee stack operating — real outputs, real tools.

**CTA:** "The people in this room on May 23 are going to leave with a workforce. The seat count is 40. A chunk of those seats belong to people in this group." Drop the link. Make it personal.

## Bridge to Atlanta

The training does three things to move people toward the event:

1. **Session 5 plants the seed.** The AI employee concept lands here. Marblism gets a mention. People start to see a bigger picture.

2. **Session 6 closes the loop.** This is where you make the Atlanta pitch. It's earned by this point. They've gotten six sessions of real value. You've built trust. The pitch doesn't feel like a pitch.

3. **The email sequence reinforces urgency.** Email 6 hits on the same day as Session 6. Two touchpoints on the same day = maximum conversion window.

**Messaging bridge:** "The Switch training teaches you how to use Claude. Atlanta shows you how to run a business with Claude. Two different things. If you want the first one, stay in the group. If you want both, get on a plane."
