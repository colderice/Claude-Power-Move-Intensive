# Top Social — Brand Voice Guide
**"We do social for you."**

---

## Brand Personality

If Top Social were a person, they'd be the most creative person in the room who actually delivers. Not just big ideas and pretty visuals — they follow through. They're the friend who built their own brand from nothing, figured out what works, and now helps other business owners skip the trial and error. Energetic without being exhausting. Creative without being flaky. Confident because the results back it up.

---

## Voice Attributes

### 1. Energetic
- **We are:** High-energy, enthusiastic, and momentum-driven. We make social feel exciting because it *is* exciting when it's working.
- **We are not:** Hyper, chaotic, or over-the-top. Energy should feel purposeful, not performative.
- **This sounds like:** "Your brand deserves to show up. Every day. With content that actually stops the scroll."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "OMG we are SO excited to share this!!! You're going to LOVE it!!!"

### 2. Creative
- **We are:** Fresh, original, and visually-minded. We think about what will actually resonate — not just what's easy to post.
- **We are not:** Trendy for the sake of it. Creativity is in service of the client's brand, not ours.
- **This sounds like:** "Cookie-cutter content gets cookie-cutter results. We build something that looks and feels like *you*."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "We use best-in-class synergistic content strategies to maximize brand storytelling."

### 3. Direct
- **We are:** Clear, confident, and to the point. Small business owners are busy. Respect that.
- **We are not:** Blunt to the point of being cold. Direct just means we don't waste their time.
- **This sounds like:** "You run the business. We run the social. Simple."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "As a comprehensive social media management solution, we provide end-to-end content lifecycle management for your organization's digital presence."

### 4. Relatable
- **We are:** Grounded in the real world of small business — the long hours, the tight budgets, the wearing-every-hat reality.
- **We are not:** Preachy, condescending, or over-explaining. We're peer-level, not teacher-to-student.
- **This sounds like:** "You didn't start your business to spend two hours a day figuring out what to post."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "Many small business owners struggle to leverage the full potential of their social media platforms."

### 5. Results-Focused
- **We are:** Outcome-obsessed. We care about what social actually does for the client's business — growth, leads, visibility.
- **We are not:** Vanity-metric chasers. Likes don't pay the bills. We talk about real business impact.
- **This sounds like:** "More eyes on your business. More people walking through your door."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "We'll boost your engagement rate and increase your follower count organically."

## Audience

**Primary:** Small business owners — local service providers, boutiques, restaurants, trades, salons, gyms, and any owner-operated business trying to grow without adding more to their plate.

**What they care about:** Getting customers, looking professional online, not wasting money, and getting time back.

**What they know:** They understand social media exists and matters. They don't know (or want to know) the algorithm, content strategy, or best posting times. They just want it handled.

**How they want to be addressed:** Like a capable business owner who made a smart decision — not like a student learning social media for the first time.

## Core Messaging Pillars

### Pillar 1 — We Do It For You
Social media management is fully handled. Not a template kit. Not advice. Done-for-you content, posting, and presence. This is the primary differentiation and should anchor all core messaging.

*"You handle your business. We handle your social."*

### Pillar 2 — Show Up Every Day
Consistency is the game. Most small businesses fall off after a few weeks. Top Social keeps clients visible, active, and relevant — without them having to think about it.

*"The algorithm rewards consistency. So does your audience."*

### Pillar 3 — Creative That Fits Your Brand
Not generic. Not recycled. Content that actually looks and sounds like the client's business — because that's what builds trust and recognition.

*"Your brand has a personality. We make sure it shows up that way online."*

### Pillar 4 — Real Results for Real Businesses
Social that works means more customers, more calls, more foot traffic. We tie our work to business outcomes — not just content performance.

*"Social that looks good is fine. Social that brings in business is the goal."*

## Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Facebook (client's page) | Warm, local, community-focused | "It's Monday. That means [client promo/update]. Come see us." |
| Top Social's own social | Energetic, confident, punchy | "Your competitors are posting. Are you?" |
| Email to prospects | Direct, conversational, benefit-led | "Here's exactly what we'd do with your Facebook page." |
| Sales page / website | Bold, clear, outcome-driven | "We do social for you. All of it." |
| Client onboarding | Friendly, clear, reassuring | "Here's how this works — it's simpler than you think." |
| Responding to leads | Fast, human, no corporate speak | "Love it. Let's talk this week — what's your schedule look like?" |

## Style Rules

| Rule | Top Social Standard |
|---|---|
| Contractions | Always use them. "We're" not "we are." |
| Sentence case | Headers in sentence case. Not Title Case For Everything. |
| Exclamation marks | One per piece, max. Only when genuinely warranted. |
| ALL CAPS | Never. Bold for emphasis if needed. |
| Emojis | On Facebook content: yes, used with purpose. In business comms: minimal. |
| Oxford comma | Yes. Always. |
| Numbers | Spell out one through nine. Numerals for 10 and above. |
| Buzzwords to avoid | "synergy," "leverage" (as a verb), "best-in-class," "end-to-end," "innovative," "cutting-edge" |

## Terminology

| Use This | Not This | Why |
|---|---|---|
| We do social for you | Social media management services | Too formal. Too generic. |
| Social content | Posts / content assets | Keep it plain |
| Show up online | Build your digital presence | Real people talk, not marketing people |
| Clients | Customers / end users | They're partners, not transactions |
| Results | Metrics / KPIs | Speaks to outcomes, not vanity |
| Facebook | Meta (when referring to the platform) | Clients say Facebook |

---

## What Top Social Never Says

- Never position the client as clueless. They're busy, not uninformed.
- Never over-promise virality or "going viral." That's not the pitch.
- Never use agency jargon. No one hired us to hear about "content pillars" and "omnichannel strategy."
- Never speak down to small business owners. They built something. Respect that.
- Never make it about us. Every sentence should be about what the client gains.

---

## The One-Sentence Brand Voice Test

Before publishing anything, ask: *Does this sound like a creative, energetic professional who actually gets small business — and gets results?*

If yes, post it. If not, rewrite it.
